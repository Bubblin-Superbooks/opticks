And if this Proportion be found true by experience it will amount to a demonstration that these Rings (like those formed in thin Plates do depend on the thickness of the Glass. I procured therefore another concavo-convex Plate of Glass ground on both sides to the same Sphere with the former Plate. Its thickness was 5/62 Parts of an Inch; and the Diameters of the three first bright Rings measured between the brightest Parts of their Orbits at the distance of six Feet from the Glass were 3·4-1/6·5-1/8· Inches.
